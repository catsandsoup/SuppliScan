# Micronutrient Compendium

Vitamins and minerals: common names, clinical names, all known forms, and measurement conventions.

---

## Unit Reference

| Unit | Full name | Scale |
|------|-----------|-------|
| g | gram | base |
| mg | milligram | 10⁻³ g |
| mcg / μg | microgram | 10⁻⁶ g |
| ng | nanogram | 10⁻⁹ g |
| IU | International Unit | biological activity; conversion varies per nutrient |
| RAE | Retinol Activity Equivalent | vitamin A potency standard |
| DFE | Dietary Folate Equivalent | adjusts for folic acid's higher bioavailability vs food folate |
| NE | Niacin Equivalent | 60 mg tryptophan converts to 1 mg NE |
| nmol/L | nanomoles per litre | serum/plasma concentration |
| pmol/L | picomoles per litre | serum/plasma concentration |
| ng/mL | nanograms per millilitre | serum/plasma concentration (= mcg/L) |
| pg/mL | picograms per millilitre | serum B12 |
| μg/L | micrograms per litre | serum trace minerals |
| mmol/L | millimoles per litre | electrolytes in serum |
| mEq/L | milliequivalents per litre | older electrolyte notation; numerically = mmol/L for monovalent ions |

## Vitamin A

**Clinical name:** Retinol (preformed) / Provitamin A carotenoids (plant-derived precursors)
**Dietary unit:** mcg RAE
**Legacy unit:** IU (1 mcg RAE = 3.33 IU retinol; 12 mcg beta-carotene from food = 1 mcg RAE)
**Serum unit:** μmol/L (serum retinol)

| Form | Type | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Retinol | Preformed, active | Primary animal-food form; free alcohol |
| Retinal (retinaldehyde) | Preformed, active | Aldehyde; intermediate in rhodopsin/phototransduction cycle |
| Retinoic acid | Preformed, active | Acid form; gene expression regulation; used clinically (tretinoin) |
| All-trans retinoic acid (ATRA) | Preformed, active | Isomer used in leukaemia treatment |
| 13-cis retinoic acid (isotretinoin) | Preformed, active | Acne pharmacotherapy |
| Retinyl palmitate | Ester/storage | Storage form in liver; used in supplements and fortification |
| Retinyl acetate | Ester/storage | Supplement form; more stable than retinol |
| Retinyl propionate | Ester/storage | Cosmetic/topical use |
| Beta-carotene (β-carotene) | Provitamin A | Most potent provitamin; 12 mcg food = 1 mcg RAE; 2 mcg supplement = 1 mcg RAE |
| Alpha-carotene (α-carotene) | Provitamin A | ~50% potency of beta-carotene; 24 mcg food = 1 mcg RAE |
| Beta-cryptoxanthin | Provitamin A | Found in orange/yellow foods; 24 mcg food = 1 mcg RAE |
| Lycopene | Carotenoid | Not a vitamin A precursor; no RAE conversion |
| Lutein | Carotenoid | Not a vitamin A precursor |
| Zeaxanthin | Carotenoid | Not a vitamin A precursor |

## Vitamin D

**Clinical name:** Calciferol
**Dietary unit:** mcg or IU (1 mcg = 40 IU)
**Serum unit:** nmol/L or ng/mL as 25-hydroxyvitamin D; thresholds vary by guideline: deficient <30 nmol/L, insufficient 30–50, sufficient 50–125

| Form | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Cholecalciferol (D3) | Synthesised in skin via UVB; found in animal foods and most supplements |
| Ergocalciferol (D2) | Plant/fungal origin; used in some prescriptions and fortification; produces lower sustained 25(OH)D than D3 at equivalent dose |
| Calcifediol / Calcidiol (25-hydroxyvitamin D3, 25(OH)D3) | Liver metabolite; primary circulating storage form; used in serum testing; available as prescription supplement in some countries |
| Calcitriol (1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D3) | Biologically active hormone; produced by kidneys; prescription only (Rocaltrol, Calcijex) |
| Ercalcidiol (25-hydroxyergocalciferol, 25(OH)D2) | D2 circulating form |
| Ercalcitriol (1,25-dihydroxyergocalciferol) | Active D2 metabolite |
| Alfacalcidol (1α-hydroxyvitamin D3) | Synthetic prohormone; requires one hydroxylation step; used in renal disease |
| Dihydrotachysterol (DHT) | Synthetic analogue; requires no renal hydroxylation; older prescription form |
| Paricalcitol | Synthetic calcitriol analogue; renal secondary hyperparathyroidism |
| Doxercalciferol | Synthetic vitamin D2 analogue; renal disease |

## Vitamin E

**Clinical name:** Tocopherol / Tocotrienol
**Dietary unit:** mg alpha-tocopherol
**Legacy unit:** IU (natural d-alpha-tocopherol: 1 mg = 1.49 IU; synthetic dl-alpha-tocopherol: 1 mg = 1.10 IU)
**Serum unit:** μmol/L or mg/dL (plasma alpha-tocopherol)

### Tocopherols (saturated phytyl side chain)

| Form | Relative activity | Notes |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| Alpha-tocopherol (α-T) | 100% | Highest biological activity; predominant form in human tissue |
| Beta-tocopherol (β-T) | ~25–50% | Less common in food |
| Gamma-tocopherol (γ-T) | ~10–35% | Dominant dietary form in Western diets (nuts, seeds, soy, corn oil) |
| Delta-tocopherol (δ-T) | ~1% | Lowest vitamin E activity of the four |

### Tocotrienols (unsaturated geranylgeranyl side chain)

| Form | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Alpha-tocotrienol (α-T3) | Palm oil, rice bran; neuroprotective research |
| Beta-tocotrienol (β-T3) | |
| Gamma-tocotrienol (γ-T3) | |
| Delta-tocotrienol (δ-T3) | |

### Supplement-specific forms

| Form | Notes |
|------|-------|
| d-alpha-tocopherol | Natural; RRR stereoisomer; higher bioavailability |

| Form | Notes |

## Vitamin B3

**Common name:** Niacin
**Clinical name:** Nicotinic acid (niacin); Nicotinamide (niacinamide)
**Dietary unit:** mg NE; 60 mg tryptophan converts to 1 mg NE
**Serum unit:** μmol/L (plasma nicotinamide); NAD+ levels in blood cells

| Form | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Nicotinic acid | Classic niacin; causes prostaglandin-mediated skin flushing; lowers LDL and triglycerides, raises HDL at pharmacological doses (1,000–3,000 mg) |
| Nicotinamide / Niacinamide | Amide form; no flushing; does not improve lipid profile at standard doses; used for skin conditions (acne, rosacea) and type 1 diabetes prevention research |
| Inositol hexanicotinate | "Flush-free niacin"; nicotinic acid releases poorly from this complex; weak evidence for niacin-equivalent activity |
| Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide (NAD+) | Active coenzyme form; central to cellular redox reactions |
| NADH | Reduced form of NAD+; used in some supplements |
| Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate (NADP+) | Phosphorylated coenzyme form |
| NADPH | Reduced NADP+; used in biosynthetic reactions and antioxidant defence |
| Nicotinamide riboside (NR) | NAD+ precursor via NRK pathway; oral bioavailability confirmed; longevity/metabolic research |
| Nicotinamide mononucleotide (NMN) | More direct NAD+ precursor than NR via NMNAT pathway; same research space |

## Vitamin B7

**Common name:** Biotin; formerly vitamin H
**Clinical name:** Biotin / D-Biotin
**Dietary unit:** mcg
**Serum unit:** nmol/L or pmol/L

| Form | Notes |
|------|-------|
| D-Biotin | Only biologically active stereoisomer |
| Biocytin (biotinyl-L-lysine) | Protein-bound form in food; requires biotinidase for hydrolysis before absorption |
| Biotinyl-AMP | Biosynthesis intermediate |

**Lab interference note:** Supplemental doses >5,000 mcg/day interfere with biotinylated immunoassay panels, producing false results in thyroid function tests (falsely low TSH, falsely high T4/T3) and high-sensitivity troponin assays. Clinically significant.

## Vitamin B9

**Common name:** Folate (food form); Folic acid (synthetic)
**Clinical name:** Pteroylglutamic acid
**Dietary unit:** mcg DFE

DFE conversions:
- 1 mcg food folate = 1 mcg DFE
- 1 mcg folic acid taken WITH food = 1.7 mcg DFE
- 1 mcg folic acid on empty stomach = 2.0 mcg DFE

**Serum unit:** nmol/L (plasma and RBC folate)

| Form | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Dietary folate (food 5-MTHF) | Reduced polyglutamate forms in food; relatively unstable to heat/light |
| Folic acid (pteroylmonoglutamic acid) | Fully oxidised synthetic form; highest stability; requires DHFR reduction before use; does not occur naturally in food |
| Dihydrofolate (DHF) | Reduction intermediate |
| Tetrahydrofolate (THF) | Core active coenzyme backbone; accepts one-carbon units |
| 5-methyltetrahydrofolate (5-MTHF) | Predominant circulating form; methyl donor in homocysteine remethylation; product of MTHFR enzyme |
| L-methylfolate / (6S)-5-MTHF | Biologically active stereoisomer of 5-MTHF; commercial forms: Metafolin (Gnosis), Quatrefolic (Gnosis), Deplin (prescription) |
| 5-formyltetrahydrofolate (folinic acid / leucovorin) | Stable active folate; bypasses DHFR and MTHFR; used clinically to counter methotrexate toxicity; calcium folinate is the supplement/prescription salt |
| 10-formyltetrahydrofolate | Active coenzyme in purine de novo synthesis |
| 5,10-methylenetetrahydrofolate | One-carbon donor in thymidylate synthesis |
| 5,10-methenyltetrahydrofolate | Interconversion intermediate |

**MTHFR note:** ~10–15% of the population carries C677T variants reducing MTHFR enzyme activity; L-methylfolate bypasses this polymorphism. Folic acid supplementation in these individuals may lead to unmetabolised folic acid accumulation in plasma.

## Vitamin B12

**Common name:** Cobalamin
**Clinical name:** Cobalamin
**Dietary unit:** mcg
**Serum unit:** pg/mL or pmol/L (serum B12); functional markers: methylmalonic acid (MMA, elevated in deficiency), homocysteine (elevated); holotranscobalamin (active B12; most sensitive early marker)

| Form | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Cyanocobalamin | Synthetic; most chemically stable; must convert to methylcobalamin and adenosylcobalamin in body; contains trace cyanide (clinically insignificant at nutritional doses; relevant in tobacco smokers and cyanide exposure) |
| Methylcobalamin | Active coenzyme form; methyl group donor in methionine synthase reaction; preferred for neurological applications |
| Adenosylcobalamin (dibencozide, coenzyme B12) | Active coenzyme form; used in mitochondria by methylmalonyl-CoA mutase |
| Hydroxocobalamin | Natural form found in food; longer half-life than cyanocobalamin; preferred for IM injection in many countries; antidote for cyanide poisoning at high doses |
| Aquacobalamin | Natural form; similar to hydroxocobalamin; converts readily to active forms |

## Nickel

**Clinical name:** Nickel (Ni); atomic number 28
**Dietary unit:** mcg; no established RDA
**Serum unit:** nmol/L or μg/L
